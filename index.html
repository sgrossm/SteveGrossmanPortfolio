About Me

Exploring the intersection of creativity and logic.

Growing up, I was exposed to musicians and engineers who kindled my passion for music and technology. My father, an electrical engineer and fantastic keyboard player, found a middle ground building modular synthesizers. I spent much of my youth playing instruments, composing digital music, creating illustrations, building electronic sound devices. After obtaining my Computer Science degree from UC Irvine, I worked as an Information Security software engineer. This industry fascinated me because it applied real-world concerns to application development. This role taught me how to balance software functionality with potential business threats. After a few years, I began searching for a career that combines technical expertise with creative expression. Playing some of my favorite games again (Oddworld, Chrono Trigger, Cuphead, Bioshock) led me to realize game development is the perfect convergence of my interests. I started to dive into the world of game engine programming, level design, audio middleware, and art pipelines. It has been an amazing journey filled with self-discovery, challenging problems, and collaborative learning, culminating in my role as software developer and audio lead at SYGO Studios. I am deeply excited to join a company where I can work alongside innovative professionals to create exciting interactive experiences.

Some of my other interests include surfing, meditation, philosophy, and cultural exploration.
